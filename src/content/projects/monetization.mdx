## Context

A Rome travel & lifestyle brand had great experiences but flat bookings. I made the product go viral with daily content, then built the attribution to prove exactly how much revenue it brought in.

## What I did

- **Made the product go viral.** I directed a daily content engine, briefing creators to ship several videos a day across accounts, owning the hooks, formats and creative direction. The product became the story, and the story drove bookings.
- **Built a funnel, not just a feed.** Videos that hooked people, content that made them plan the trip, and a quick path from watching to booking.
- **Ran the offer like a growth loop.** Time-boxed promo codes created urgency; the best-performing hooks were re-cut and re-shipped across accounts within days.
- **Tied every euro back to a video.** Promo codes per campaign, UTM-tagged links, GA4 and Rezdy as the booking system of record, so bookings traced to the content that drove them. Reported weekly in revenue, not vanity metrics.

## Result

**€80K+ in direct, social-attributed bookings per year.** Two viral videos booked **€40K in 48 hours**; a single 3.1M-view video drove **€23K**, and a 2.5M-view video another **€17K**, all tracked through to Rezdy, not estimated.
